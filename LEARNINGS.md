## Patterns observed (what to lean on / avoid)
- **Favourite vs World-Cup debutant → goals flow.** Germany 7-1 Curaçao, Canada 6-0 Qatar, Switzerland 4-1 Bosnia, Sweden 5-1 Tunisia. Over 1.5 (and often 2.5) is strong here.
- **Blowouts run CLEAN and LOW-FOUL.** Germany 7-1 Curaçao had **0 cards** and **11 fouls** — this is why the "6×€2" Bet Builder (over 1.5 cards + over 15.5 fouls) LOST. Lesson: in a one-sided game, do NOT bet over-cards or over-fouls; lean on goals/corners instead.
- **Debutants who park the bus can frustrate for a half.** Spain 0-0 Cape Verde — even elite favourites can be held when the opponent defends deep and the favourite is wasteful. Keep goal lines modest (1.5, not 2.5) when the underdog is purely defensive.
- **Two strong, open sides → over 1.5 reliable.** England 4-2 Croatia, France 3-1 Senegal, Iraq 1-4 Norway.
- **Same-match multi-market combos are often unplaceable** in a normal accumulator (e.g. "over 1.5 + DNB"). Use single markets across different matches, or a Bet Builder priced as one.

## Strategy notes
- **Single market per match, Over 1.5 goals** has been the most robust ~1.40 leg (80% base rate this tournament).
- Compounding long chains stays high-variance regardless of per-leg quality — one upset ends the run. Stake only disposable money.
